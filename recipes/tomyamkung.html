Tomyam Kung

Description

Thailand’s most iconic soup — clear, aromatic, and intensely flavorful. Built on a fragrant broth infused with lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves, it delivers a sharp interplay of sourness and heat. Juicy prawns add natural sweetness, while fresh herbs give the soup its unmistakable brightness and clarity.

On the palate, it is light yet powerful. Citrus notes cut through the chili heat, balanced by savory depth and herbal freshness. Clean, invigorating, and precise, Tom Yum Kung is a masterclass in balance and restraint.

Ingredients

  • Fresh prawns (shrimp)
  • Lemongrass
  • Galangal
  • Kaffir lime leaves
  • Bird's eye chilies
  • Lime juice
  • Fish sauce
  • Mushrooms
  • Coriander (cilantro)

Steps

  1. Boil water or stock with lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves until aromatic.
  2. Add mushrooms and chilies, simmer briefly.
  3. Add prawns and cook just until tender.
  4. Season with fish sauce and lime juice (off heat).
  5. Garnish with coriander and serve hot.
